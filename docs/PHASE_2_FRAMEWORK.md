# Phase 2: Framework Design
India State Competitiveness Index (ISCI)

Version 1.0
Date: 11 July 2026

---

## Purpose

This document adapts Porter's Diamond framework to the level of an Indian state. It
explains what each part of the framework means for a state, so that indicator
selection in Phase 3 has a clear structure to work against. It defines the
framework only. It does not choose indicators and involves no code.

## The framework in short

Porter's Diamond has four determinants that shape the competitive environment:
factor conditions, demand conditions, related and supporting industries, and firm
strategy, structure and rivalry. Two external forces act on all four: government
and chance. Government is treated as an influence on the four determinants, not as
a fifth determinant. Chance covers outside shocks that the model acknowledges but
does not measure.

## The four determinants at state level

### Factor conditions

The stock of inputs a state can draw on for production. Porter groups these into
broad areas such as human resources, physical infrastructure, capital, and
knowledge. What matters is not only whether these factors exist, but how well
developed and specialised they are. In plain terms, how ready the ground is for
economic activity.

### Demand conditions

The nature of demand within the state, not only its size. Two things matter
together: how large the home market is, and how sophisticated and demanding local
customers are. Demanding buyers push firms to raise quality, innovate, and become
more productive, so the character of local demand can matter as much as its
volume.

### Related and supporting industries

Whether a state has capable suppliers and related industries that reinforce one
another. When connected industries and their supply chains are present and strong,
often clustered together, firms gain support, faster information, and shared
capabilities they could not build alone.

### Firm strategy, structure and rivalry

The conditions that govern how firms are created, organised and managed in the
state, and the nature of competition among them. Porter's argument is that strong
local rivalry, rather than a quiet or protected market, pushes firms to improve
and tends to raise quality and productivity over time.

## The two external influences

### Government

Government shapes all four determinants through policy, the quality of public
spending, public investment, and the state's own finances. It is an influencing
factor, not a core determinant, and the analysis treats it that way.

### Chance

Outside events and shocks that fall outside the model. We note them where relevant
but do not try to measure them in the index.

## What comes next

This framework sets the structure. In Phase 3 we select the specific indicators for
each determinant, drawing only on the sources cleared in the Phase 0 audit, and we
record each indicator's source, table, and year so every number can be traced.
