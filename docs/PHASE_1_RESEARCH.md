# Phase 1: Research and Business Understanding
India State Competitiveness Index (ISCI)

Version 1.0
Date: 11 July 2026

---

## Purpose

This document sets the research foundation for the project. It states what we mean
by state competitiveness, what we are trying to do, and the questions the index
must answer. Every later choice, from indicator selection to the final paper,
follows from what is written here.

## Working definition

A state's competitiveness is its ability to create an environment where people,
businesses and institutions can use available resources productively, encourage
innovation, generate sustainable economic growth, and improve the standard of
living over time.

This definition serves as the operational definition used throughout the project.

A note on framing. This project uses Michael Porter's approach from The
Competitive Advantage of Nations. Porter does not treat competitiveness and
productivity as the same thing. He treats productivity as the primary driver of
competitiveness: a place is competitive when its firms, workers and institutions
are productive, and when the conditions around them keep raising that
productivity. Porter's Diamond framework describes those conditions. So we measure
the environment that enables productivity, not output on its own.

## Research objective

To design and build a transparent, reproducible index that measures and compares
the competitiveness of Indian states using official data, organised around
Porter's Diamond framework, and to identify and explain the factors behind
differences in state competitiveness.

The objective is what we set out to do. The research question, below, is what we
set out to answer. They are kept separate on purpose.

## Primary research question

Why do some Indian states perform better than others in creating a competitive
environment, and which factors explain these differences?

## Supporting research questions

1. Which dimensions of Porter's Diamond Framework explain the observed
   differences in state competitiveness, and what role does government play in
   shaping those conditions?
2. How efficiently do states convert available resources into stronger economic
   and social outcomes?
3. How much do factor conditions such as infrastructure, labour, and access to
   finance explain differences in competitiveness?
4. Do states with a stronger business and industrial base (MSME registrations,
   factories, investment) tend to rank higher?
5. How stable are state rankings over time, and are the gaps between leading and
   lagging states widening or narrowing?

Note: questions 2 to 5 depend on the indicators we select in Phase 3 and on the
years available in the sources. Question 5 needs multi-year data in particular.
The NSDP series and several RBI State tables provide it, but we confirm this
indicator by indicator before relying on it.

## Initial Research Hypotheses

These are initial hypotheses that will be tested during the project. They are not
conclusions.

H1. States with stronger factor conditions tend to have higher competitiveness.
H2. A stronger industrial and MSME base is associated with higher competitiveness.
H3. Better factor conditions are associated with higher productivity.
H4. Differences in competitiveness cannot be explained by income alone.
H5. Competitiveness is influenced by multiple dimensions rather than a single
indicator.
