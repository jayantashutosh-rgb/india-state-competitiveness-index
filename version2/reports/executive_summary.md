# India State Competitiveness Index (ISCI)
## Executive Summary — Version 2.0

### What this is

The India State Competitiveness Index (ISCI) measures how competitive Indian states are for
business, using only official government data. It gives each state a single score, ranks the
states, and explains what the ranking means through Michael Porter's Diamond idea.

### How it is built

The data comes from two official sources: the RBI Handbook of Statistics on Indian States
2024-25 and the MSME Annual Report. From these, 11 indicators are used. They cover two parts
of Porter's Diamond: basic conditions (schooling, health, jobs, power, roads) and industry
(factories, small businesses, manufacturing, investment).

Every indicator is put on a 0 to 1 scale and counts the same. A state's score is the average
of its indicators. States with too little data are left out. In the end, 33 states and union
territories get a score. The ranking was tested and is stable.

### Main results

- Scores run from about 0.20 to 0.61. Goa is the highest, Arunachal Pradesh the lowest. The
  scores are close together, so the order matters more than the gaps.
- States fall into three groups: strong on both parts (12), strong basic conditions but
  weaker industry (6), and weak on both parts (15).
- Against the national average, 23 of 33 states have their biggest weakness in basic
  conditions.
- Against the strongest states, 26 of 33 states have their biggest gap in industry, and
  industry is the main priority area for 26 of 33 states.
- If a state closes its single biggest gap, its score rises a little and its rank moves by 0
  to 6 places. Weak states stay near the bottom.

### What it means

Basic conditions explain where a state stands against the average today. Industry explains
the distance to the strongest states. Most states would need gains in more than one area to
move much further up.

### Limits

The index measures two of Porter's four parts. It uses only official data and does not
include every factor that affects development. The scenarios are simple simulations, not
forecasts. The findings show links in the data, not cause.

### Next steps

A later version could add measures for the other two parts of Porter's Diamond (local demand
and firm competition), add more indicators, and update the unemployment measure if a combined
state figure is published.

### Sources

RBI, Handbook of Statistics on Indian States 2024-25; Ministry of MSME, Annual Report.
